Discovery Call Script
Rexora Systems
15-20 min · Diagnose, don't pitch

1 · Opening 2 min

Goal: Set tone. Build rapport. Set expectations.

  • "Hi {{Name}}, thanks for making time."
  • "Quick agenda: I want to learn about your school for ~10 min, then I'll show you what we'd recommend. Sound good?"
  • "Before we dive in — anything specific you were hoping to get out of this call?"

2 · Current state 4 min

Goal: Understand what they have today.

  • "How many students do you currently have?"
  • "Walk me through how you collect school fees today — start to finish."
  • "How do parents currently get updates from the school? (calls, WhatsApp, paper letters?)"
  • "Do you have a website? How often do you update it?"
  • "What systems are you using now — Excel, paper, or any software?"

3 · Surface the pain 4 min

Goal: Get them to articulate what's broken. They sell themselves.

  • "What's the most time-consuming part of running the school?"
  • "How much time does your bursar spend chasing unpaid fees each week?"
  • "How often do parents call asking the same questions?"
  • "What happens when a parent loses a receipt or report card?"
  • "If you could fix ONE thing about how the school runs admin-wise, what would it be?"

Listen for: hours wasted, frustrated parents, late fees, manual errors, lost data.

4 · Paint the future 3 min

Goal: Get them visualizing the solution.

  • "Imagine parents pay fees from their phone in 30 seconds — and you get notified instantly. What would that change for the bursar?"
  • "Imagine every parent has a login to see their child's attendance, fees, and reports — no more calls. What's that worth weekly?"
  • "Imagine the system auto-sends fee reminders 7 days before due. How much late payment would that recover?"

Then say: "Want me to show you that working right now?" → Share the demo link.

5 · Position the investment 3 min

Goal: Anchor on value before saying the price. Then say the price calmly.

Before stating price, say:

"Most schools we work with save 8-10 staff hours per week, recover 15-25% more on-time fees, and increase admission inquiries by ~30% in the first term. The investment ranges from ₦150k to ₦750k depending on what's included. Want me to walk through what fits {{School Name}}?"

6 · Handle objections

"It's too expensive"
"Totally fair — let me ask: what's the cost of your bursar spending 10 hours/week chasing fees? At even ₦500/hour, that's ₦240k/year in wasted time alone. The system pays for itself in year 1, then saves money every year after."
"Send us a proposal first"
"Happy to. Quick question to scope it right: are you looking at this term, or planning for next term? That tells me the timeline I should propose."
"We need to discuss internally"
"Of course. Who else needs to be in that conversation? Want me to join a quick call to answer their questions directly? Saves you having to repeat everything."
"We already have someone building this"
"Got it. How's that going? When are they delivering? Happy to be plan B if anything slips — I'll stay in touch."
"Parents won't use a portal — they're not tech-savvy"
"This is the most common worry. We design for that — WhatsApp notifications, USSD payments, no-app-required portal. 92% of parents in our demos used it without training."
"Can you do it for ₦80,000?"
"I can't do the full package at that price — but I CAN do the Starter package for ₦150k, and we can add features later as the school grows. Want to start there?"

7 · Close the call 2 min

Goal: Lock the next step. Never end a call without a clear next action.

The 3 possible exits:

  • 🟢 Hot: "Great — I'll send the proposal within 24 hours. Best email? And let's hold a slot for next Thursday to walk through it together — does {{time}} work?"
  • 🟡 Warm: "Sounds like timing might be {{next term/next year}}. Mind if I check in with you in {{X weeks}}? I'll also send the demo link + a 1-pager you can show the team."
  • 🔴 Cold: "All good — sounds like it's not the right fit right now. If anything changes, you have my number. Mind if I follow up in 6 months in case priorities shift?"

Whatever happens — within 1 hour, send a thank-you WhatsApp/email with the demo link + a summary of what they said. This converts more deals than anything else.

✅ DO

  • Use their school's name in every response
  • Take notes on screen so they SEE you listening
  • Pause for 3 seconds after a question — let them think
  • Quote their exact words back ("you said admins waste 10 hours/week...")
  • Share the demo screen if you're on Zoom
  • End with a clear next step + timeline

❌ DON'T

  • Start pitching before you've heard their pain
  • Talk about features — talk about outcomes
  • Apologize for pricing or discount unprompted
  • Use jargon (API, backend, framework, deploy)
  • Promise things you can't deliver in 6 weeks
  • Leave the call without a next step locked in
